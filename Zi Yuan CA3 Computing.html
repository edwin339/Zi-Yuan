How Can You Tell If It's a Scam?

Scams are becoming increasingly common and convincing online. More people fall victim to them than you might expect! This webpage is designed to help you recognize different types of scams, avoid falling for them, and assist others in staying safe too.

Types of Online Scams

    • Phishing Scams: Fake emails or messages designed to steal personal information.
    • Online Shopping Scams: Fraudulent websites or sellers who never deliver goods.
    • Tech Support Scams: Impersonators claiming to fix fake computer issues.
    • Romance Scams: Scammers use fake profiles to build relationships and request money.
    • Investment & Crypto Scams: Fake opportunities promising high returns.
    • Lottery or Prize Scams: Claims of winning prizes that require upfront fees.

    Common Examples of Online Scams

    • An email from a “bank” asking you to verify your login credentials.
    • A pop-up warning you of a virus, prompting you to call tech support.
    • A message from a social media “friend” asking for emergency money.
    • A text message claiming you’ve won a prize and asking for personal info.

    Tips to Prevent and Avoid Scams

    • Verify the Source: Don’t click on unknown links or attachments.
    • Check for Red Flags: Look for bad grammar, urgency, or strange email addresses.
    • Use Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Add extra protection to your accounts.
    • Don’t Share Personal Information: Never give out sensitive data via email or chat.
    • Research Before You Buy: Verify sellers and read reviews before purchases.

    Recognizing and Avoiding Common Online Scams

    Type of Scam Common Signs How to Avoid
    Phishing Scams Emails or texts asking for login info or clicking suspicious links Check sender email, don’t click unknown links, use spam filters
    Online Shopping Scams Unrealistic prices, no reviews, fake websites Buy from trusted sites, verify sellers, read reviews
    Tech Support Scams Pop-ups warning of viruses, fake tech support phone numbers Don’t call unknown numbers, close the browser, scan with antivirus
    Romance Scams Online “partners” asking for money, avoiding video calls Be cautious with online relationships, never send money
    Crypto/Investment Scams Guaranteed high returns, urgent pressure to invest Research platforms, ignore “too good to be true” offers
    Impersonation Scams Messages from “friends” or “officials” asking for money or help Contact the person using another method to confirm identity
    Job Scams Fake jobs requiring upfront fees or personal info Look up the company, avoid any job that asks for money
